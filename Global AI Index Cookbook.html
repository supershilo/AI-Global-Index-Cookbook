üîóData Source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/katerynameleshenko/ai-index

üìå The Global AI Index includes seven indicators affecting the Index on 62 countries, as well as general information about the countries (region, cluster, income group and political regime). The Global AI Index is the first index to benchmark nations on their level of investment, innovation and implementation of artificial intelligence.

üõ†Ô∏è Implementation - represents the application of artificial intelligence by professionals in various sectors, such as businesses, governments, and communities.

  • Talent indicator focuses on the availability of skilled practitioners for the provision of artificial intelligence solutions.

  • Infrastructure indicator focuses on the reliability and scale of access infrastructure, from electricity and internet, to super computing capabilities.

  • Operating Environment indicator focuses on the regulatory context, and public opinion surrounding artificial intelligence.

üí° Innovation - reflects the progress made in technology and methodology, which signify the potential for artificial intelligence to evolve and improve.

  • Research indicator focuses on the extent of specialist research and researchers; investigating the amount of publications and citations in credible academic journals.

  • Development indicator focuses on the development of fundamental platforms and algorithms upon which innovative artificial intelligence projects rely.

üí∏ Investment - reflects financial and procedural commitments to artificial intelligence

  • Government Strategy indicator focuses on the depth of commitment from national government to artificial intelligence; investigating spending commitments and national strategies.

  • Commercial indicator focuses on the level of startup activity, investment and business initiatives based on artificial intelligence.

üìä Data Columns:

  1. Country: The name of the country or nation being evaluated in the dataset.
  2. Talent: A measure of the country's talent pool or human resources related to AI, indicating the quality and quantity of AI professionals and experts.
  3. Infrastructure: An assessment of the country's AI-related infrastructure, which includes factors like technological capabilities, research facilities, and computing resources.
  4. Operating Environment: An evaluation of the overall business and regulatory environment for AI within the country, including factors that impact AI adoption and development.
  5. Research: An assessment of the country's contributions to AI research, including the quality and quantity of research papers, patents, and academic institutions involved in AI research.
  6. Development: A measure of the country's AI development activities, encompassing factors like innovation, startups, and the development of AI technologies and applications.
  7. Government Strategy: An evaluation of the government's strategic initiatives and policies related to AI, which can significantly impact the growth and direction of AI within the country.
  8. Commercial: An assessment of the country's commercial AI ecosystem, including the presence of AI companies, market opportunities, and the business environment for AI-related ventures.
  9. Total Score: The cumulative score or ranking that summarizes the country's performance across all the mentioned factors, providing an overall assessment of its AI capabilities.
  10. Region: The geographical region to which the country belongs (e.g., Americas, Asia-Pacific, Europe, Middle East, Africa).
  11. Cluster: A grouping or classification of the country based on its AI performance or characteristics (e.g., Power players, Traditional champions, Rising stars, Waking up, Nascent).
  12. Income Group: The income group to which the country belongs (e.g., High income, Upper middle income, Lower middle income).
  13. Political Regime: The political regime or system of governance in the country (e.g., Liberal democracy, Closed autocracy, Electoral democracy).
